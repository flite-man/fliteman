97% of India is missing from the markets.

Stock Markets can turn out to be the single biggest wealth creators in your portfolio.

They could potentially help you earn more than Fixed Deposits and more than Real Estate if you invest wisely.

More importantly, if you measure effort or risk / reward ratio, nothing compares to the markets.

Markets need only 3 skills - Due Diligence, patience, and emotional stability.

Yet, only 3% of India invests in the markets. Here are 5 reasons why this may be:

1. They do not have disposable income.
2. They are often in debt and/or in need of loans.
3. They do not have the discipline or the incentive to save regularly.
4. They do not have emergency funds.
5. They think of markets as complex, unreliable, and not for them. They find opening an FD/RD or buying land easier in comparison.

We need solutions for all these issues hindering the remaining 97% from entering the markets.

Here are a few worth considering:

1. Pay more/ Earn more

- Employers should pay more than the "market price" for a job. The bottomline is less important if your employees are unhappy and/or underpaid. Plan a reasonably better raise year on year before you plan your next foreign vacation.

- Small businesses/Startups should add more value and aim to charge/earn more. In a "who is the cheapest" game, everyone is a loser.

2. See Solution 1

- Paying/Earning more helps clear debt, reduces dependency on loans, and incentivizes them to save and/or invest more regularly.

3. Teach India

- Help friends and/or people of service open DEMAT accounts. Gift them a stock. Teach them what you know. Guide them on the 3 key principles of investing - due diligence, patience, and emotional stability.

These simple steps should help result in at least a 1% increase in people participation in the markets. It may seem small, but that's 1 crore Indians, just for perspective.
